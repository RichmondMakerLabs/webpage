How to Join?

Richmond MakerLabs is for people with an interest in DIY and craft. We want to hear from you if you are interested in carpentry, biohacking, chemistry, gardening, knitting... We are all-inclusive! RML could also be considered as a digitally-enabled arts and crafts club, hackspace, a hackerspace, a makerspace, a making laboratory, and a making community. Do you need help with anything electrical, mechanical or technical? Or can you offer such help? Contact us or just drop in to a meeting!

Access and membership is free but we accept donations and some materials will need to be paid for. Chat with us about how to do this.

Plan your first visit

By far the best thing to do is to come visit. View Larger Map

But we also have a great wiki-page with lots of extra information maybe have a look at the stuff we have been doing in our blog.

We appreciate it's sometimes uncomfortable to walk into a room of people and introduce yourself. Some of these points may help you understand what to expect.

  • First off, just come down to see us and have a look around, no forms to fill out before.
  • We do ask that anyone under 18 is accompanied by an adult.
  • Check we're open: it is worth popping a message in our Telegram group
  • Have a look through our Facilities section to see if there's anything we have that takes your interest.
  • Someone will be able to welcome you and give you a tour - but it's done on a first come, first served basis so you may have to wait a short period.
  • It's worth noting that some tools need training before use. Whether we're able to do that on the night is largely down to who is available, but if you're aiming to use something specific please feel free to ask through methods below
  • You'll probably get the most out of your first session if you come with something you want to achieve - but it's no problem if you just want to come and say hello and get a sense of what's there. Just be aware that, whilst people can often help with advice on how to do things, we don't currently have projects to suggest.
  • Most importantly, people are friendly and please feel free to wander round and ask people what sorts of things they're working on - they'll usually be keen to show you.
